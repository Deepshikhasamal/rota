Cloth collection drive

Every year we organize a cloth collection drive in which we approach students, professors and other staff members of our institute to donate as much wearable clothes as possible. Many of us have more than what we really need. In India, itâ€™s a common tradition to give old clothes, leftover food, old notebooks, boxes etc to domestic helps. Blanket distribution camp

Money collected from the money raising events like Housie and V-day are used for buying new blankets for poor. We buy new blankets from the funds. We donate the blankets to the poor sleeping at the roadside and near Rourkela railway station. In the freezing night, when the entire Rourkela city dons their sweaters, monkey caps, mufflers with a hot cup of tea, there are few under-previleged who cannot afford even a blanket and die- without a roof or clothes on their bare bodies. It is not about changing the lives of the people but doing something even more basic than that-making them survive-enabling them to fight their battle of survival against mother nature.

Welcome to Rotaract

Rotaract is a service club for students of National Institute of Technology,Rourkela who are dedicated to the community and international service. It is one of the oldest clubs in NIT Rourkela which is the youth wing of Rotary International, which is an international service organization whose stated purpose is to bring together business and professional leaders in order to provide humanitarian services, encourage high ethical standards in all vocations, and to advance goodwill and peace around the world. It is a non-political and non-sectarian organization open to all people regardless of race, color, creed, religion, gender, or political preference.. The members work together for the eradication of various social and management skills by organizing and participating in various social events.

Rotaract (which stands for Rotary in Action) is a Rotary-sponsored service club for young men and women (ages 18 to 30). Rotaract clubs are either community or university based, and theyâ€™re sponsored by a local Rotary club. This makes them true "partners in service". The first Rotaract club was the Rotaract Club of North Charlotte in North Carolina, founded on March 13, 1968. Since then, Rotaract has grown into one of Rotary's most significant and fastest-growing service programs, with more than 9,723 clubs in about 170 countries and geographical areas comprised of over 207,690 members.Rotaract clubs are part of a global effort to bring peace and international understanding to the world. This effort starts at the community level but knows no limits in its outreach. Rotaractors have access to the many resources of Rotary International (RI) and The Rotary Foundation. Rotary International provides the administrative support that helps Rotaract clubs thrive.Rotaract is divided into districts around the world. These districts follow the Rotary-structured districts. At the district level, the District Rotaract Representative serves as the leader and representative of all clubs in their respective district to Rotary International. Each Rotaract club itself is structured differently. The Standard Club Constitution states that there is a president, vice president, secretary, and treasurer, with additional officers provided in the by-laws.
